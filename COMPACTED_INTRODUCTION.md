# Compacted Introduction

Dissolved oxygen (DO) concentration constitutes the most critical water-quality parameter in aquaculture operations, where maintaining DO within species-specific thresholds (typically 5–9 mg/L) directly governs aerobic metabolic rates, feed conversion efficiency, immunological resistance, and organism survival [1]. While conventional DO management relies on electrochemical or luminescence-based sensors paired with manual aeration scheduling, autonomous closed-loop control systems—integrating continuous sensing, embedded intelligence, and actuator-level precision—offer compelling advances in operational efficiency, economic viability, and environmental sustainability. In this context, Brushless Direct Current (BLDC) aerators modulated through a 12-bit Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC) represent an energy-efficient actuation platform whose precise governance demands a high-performance, embedded-deployable control architecture. Adaptive Neuro-Fuzzy Inference Systems (ANFIS), introduced by Jang [2], fuse the linguistic interpretability of fuzzy logic with the parametric learning capacity of neural networks, yielding a universal approximator framework well-suited to nonlinear process control under noisy measurement conditions. Early ANFIS implementations used fixed-shape membership functions (MFs) with optimization limited to consequent parameters, but subsequent work by Ying et al. [3] and Buckley and Hayashi [4] demonstrated that fully trainable premise parameters significantly improve approximation accuracy on nonlinear problems. The Generalized Bell-Shaped MF was introduced to provide a richer shape family than the Gaussian while remaining differentiable [5]; however, both Gaussian and Bell-Shaped MFs enforce symmetric, unimodal profiles centered at a single point, which may insufficiently capture skewed or multi-modal fuzzy partitions. Efforts to design more expressive MFs include the asymmetric Gaussian where separate left/right widths are learned independently [6], sigmoidal MF and its extensions [7], Cauchy MF for heavy-tailed membership [8], and spline-based MFs using piecewise polynomial representations [9]; more recent work has explored deep learning–inspired MF parameterizations that embed trainable nonlinear activation shapes [10], yet none of these proposals unify power-law scaling, exponential decay, and hyperbolic modulation in a single analytical expression with provably smooth differentiability for backpropagation. The central design degree of freedom in ANFIS is the membership function: the mathematical kernel mapping crisp scalar inputs to fuzzy degrees of membership in each linguistic partition, whose family governs the expressiveness of the rule base, the optimization landscape of premise parameter learning, and the model's ability to generalize across heterogeneous input conditions. Despite the pivotal role of MF design, the literature has been dominated by Gaussian and Generalized Bell-Shaped MFs [2], [5], both imposing center-symmetric, unimodal profiles that are structurally incapable of representing the asymmetric, position-dependent, and heavy-tailed membership landscapes arising in real DO dynamics perturbed by biological oxygen demand (BOD) variations, reaeration kinetics, temperature excursions, power-line interference (PLI), sensor quantization, impulse spikes, and supply-voltage ripple. The integration of ANFIS with proportional-integral-derivative (PID) controllers has been extensively studied for process control applications; Mann et al. [11] proposed a hybrid ANFIS-PID tuning approach where ANFIS infers optimal gain schedules based on process error signals, Wai and Muthler [12] demonstrated ANFIS-PID speed control in induction motor drives, and for biological process control, ANFIS-based aeration management has been reported by Vanrolleghem and Lee [13], who noted the particular challenge of DO dynamics' nonlinearity and the inadequacy of classical PID at varying load conditions. Aquaculture DO regulation is a well-established research problem [14], [15], where Zhou et al. [16] demonstrated the superiority of fuzzy logic controllers over classical PID for pond aeration and Chen and Novy [17] used neural network PID tuners for DO control in recirculating aquaculture systems; however, most reported systems either rely on simulation data, use simple feed-forward neural networks without a fuzzy layer, or employ large models impractical for embedded Raspberry Pi–class hardware. Sensor signal contamination in precision aquaculture includes drift (slow baseline shift), impulse noise (transient spikes), power-line interference (PLI, 50/60 Hz harmonics), quantization noise (ADC resolution limits), ripple artifacts (power supply fluctuations), and additive White Gaussian Noise (WGN) [18]; while Kalman filter-based methods are commonly used for noise mitigation [19], for the controller design problem the model must exhibit robustness across all noise regimes simultaneously—a multi-condition generalization challenge not addressed by prior ANFIS-based DO controllers. This paper addresses this structural limitation through the following specific contributions: We derive and formalize the PsiOmega Membership Function (PsiOmega MF), a novel five-parameter fuzzy kernel defined by: μⱼ(x) = \frac{{|x\ -\ c_j|}^{\alpha_j}\ exp\ (-\beta_j{|x\ -\ c_j|}^{\gamma_j})}{1\ +\ \delta_j\ arcosh\ (1\ +\ x^2)} This formulation uniquely decouples a Weibull-type power-law numerator (governing local, center-relative membership rise and decay) from an inverse hyperbolic cosine denominator that provides global, input-position-dependent saturation. The resulting "local-global factorization" confers structurally inherent asymmetry and position-aware normalization absent from all prior parametric MF families [20]. We establish the analytical properties of ψΩ: μⱼ is continuous for αⱼ>0 (assuming βⱼ, γⱼ finite) and is differentiable everywhere, including at x=cⱼ, provided αⱼ>1. In the numerator-only limit (equivalently δⱼ→0) the interior peak of the numerator occurs at |x-cⱼ|=(αⱼ/(βⱼγⱼ))^(1/γⱼ); when δⱼ≠0 the denominator Dⱼ(x)=1+δⱼ arcosh(1+x²) generally shifts this location and introduces structural asymmetry whenever cⱼ≠0 (note arcosh(1+x²) is even in x, so symmetry is recovered when cⱼ=0 or δⱼ=0). To avoid denominator singularities for all real x we recommend constraining δⱼ≥0 (or otherwise enforcing Dⱼ(x)>0). Full gradient derivations for the five learnable parameters αⱼ, βⱼ, γⱼ, δⱼ, cⱼ are provided for backpropagation; practitioners should note these gradients contain terms proportional to ln|x-cⱼ| and 1/|x-cⱼ|, so numerical regularization (e.g. t←max(|x-cⱼ|,ε)) and positive-parameter reparameterization (soft-plus or exponential) are recommended in implementation. We embed PsiOmega MFs within an integrated ANFIS-PID controller—where the ANFIS core learns both the baseline DAC voltage mapping and adaptive PID gain schedules (Kₚ, Kᵢ, Kd) simultaneously—trained end-to-end via AdamW optimization with cosine annealing learning rate scheduling. We conduct the most comprehensive empirical evaluation reported to date for ANFIS-based DO control: a 560,000-sample real-hardware dataset (420,000 training samples, 140,000 held-out test samples) spanning seven distinct noise regimes (Clean, Drift, Impulse, PLI, Quantization, Ripple, WGN), benchmarked against identically architected Gaussian-MF and Generalized Bell-Shaped-MF ANFIS-PID models in a controlled ablation study. We demonstrate that PsiOmega achieves consistent best performance across all primary and secondary metrics: control (IAE, ISE, ITAE, ITSE, settling time, steady-state error), regression (RMSE, R², CCC, NSE, Willmott d), prediction uncertainty (PICP, PIW), and computational efficiency (inference latency, parameter count, model size). The unified architecture where ANFIS simultaneously learns both the baseline plant mapping and the adaptive PID gains—as adopted in the present work—provides a differentiable, end-to-end trainable framework that has not been previously explored with a novel kernel specifically designed for this purpose, and the present system addresses all three limitations (simulation data, simple feed-forward networks, large models) through a lightweight (2,023-parameter), fully differentiable ANFIS-PID operating on real hardware-collected data at 0.042 ms/sample inference latency, trained and evaluated across all seven noise regime types to provide a significantly more comprehensive empirical validation than any previously reported DO control study.
